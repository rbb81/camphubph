# User Personas

Four personas grounded in the target user description: people who love camping, search for locations, discover new destinations, share experiences, upload photos, review campsites, find camping buddies, join communities, and plan future trips. Each persona represents a distinct primary motivation and a distinct cluster of features.

---

## 1. The Weekend Warrior

**Name:** Jasmine, 27, marketing associate in Quezon City
**Camping experience:** Beginner–intermediate, camps roughly once a month with a small group of friends

**Goals**
- Find a camp within 2–3 hours of Metro Manila that fits her budget and group size.
- Avoid trip-planning mistakes (bad weather, closed sites, unclear directions).
- See real, recent photos and honest reviews before committing.

**Frustrations**
- Existing info is scattered across Facebook Groups, blogs, and word of mouth — hard to compare options.
- Unclear what's actually at a site (facilities, terrain, price) until she's already there.

**Behaviors**
- Browses Discover by category ("Weekend Getaways", "Budget Friendly") before a trip.
- Uses Search and Map to shortlist 2–3 camps, checks Reviews and Photo Gallery.
- Saves camps to a Wishlist and revisits before booking with friends.

**Key features used:** Discover, Search, Map, Camp Details, Reviews, Saved Camps, Wishlist

**Quote:** *"I just want to know: is it safe, is it nice, and can my budget handle it — before I commit a whole Saturday to finding out."*

## 2. The Trip Reporter

**Name:** Rico, 34, freelance photographer, based in Cavite

**Camping experience:** Advanced; camps 2–3 times a month, often solo or with a rotating crew

**Goals**
- Share high-quality photos and detailed trip reports to build a personal following.
- Get recognized as a reliable source for a specific niche (e.g. sunrise/sunset spots, less-touristy trails).
- Give back useful, specific tips (gear, timing, route) to the community.

**Frustrations**
- Generic social apps bury outdoor content among unrelated posts; no dedicated audience for camping content.
- No good way to organize his own trip history and photos by location.

**Behaviors**
- Posts multi-photo trip reports with detailed captions; tags location and hashtags.
- Answers questions in comments; builds a follower base.
- Maintains a rich Profile: photo gallery, completed trips, camping experience level, favorite styles.

**Key features used:** Create Post (multi-photo), Home Feed, Profile (photos, completed trips), Follow/Mentions/Hashtags, Notifications

**Quote:** *"I want people to see the actual view, the actual trail conditions — not a stock photo — before they go."*

---

## 3. The Community Builder / Moderator

**Name:** Ate Baby (Isabel), 41, runs a regional overlanding group

**Camping experience:** Veteran camper and organizer; has run a 5,000+ member Facebook Group for years

**Goals**
- Bring her existing community into a purpose-built space with better tools (rules, pinned posts, moderation) than a generic Facebook Group.
- Keep discussions on-topic and welcoming to new members.
- Surface official info (rules, meetup schedules, safety guidelines) clearly.

**Frustrations**
- Facebook Groups mix announcements with noise; pinned posts get buried; no real moderation tooling.
- Hard to tell active, trustworthy members from spam accounts.

**Behaviors**
- Creates and manages a Community ("Overlanding PH"): sets rules, pins key posts, reviews member posts.
- Monitors reports and takes moderator actions.
- Cross-promotes events and camping news to members.

**Key features used:** Communities (create/manage, rules, pinned posts, moderators, members), Community Feed, Report handling, Events

**Quote:** *"I don't need another Facebook Group. I need a space where the rules are visible, the good posts don't get buried, and I can actually moderate."*

---

## 4. The Planner

**Name:** Miguel, 30, works in IT, plans trips for a friend group of 6–8

**Camping experience:** Intermediate; the designated "trip organizer" of his friend group

**Goals**
- Plan trips weeks in advance: pick a destination, set dates, coordinate who's coming.
- Keep a shared checklist so nothing gets forgotten (gear, food, permits).
- Look back on past trips to reuse good itineraries and remember what worked.

**Frustrations**
- Trip coordination currently happens across group chats and spreadsheets, disconnected from where he actually discovers camps.
- Hard to convert "someday" destinations (wishlist) into an actual planned trip.

**Behaviors**
- Moves camps from Wishlist into a new Trip Planner entry.
- Sets travel dates, invites friends, builds a checklist, tracks trip status (planning → confirmed → completed).
- Reviews Completed Trips later, sometimes leaving a Review right after.

**Key features used:** Trip Planner (destinations, dates, invite friends, checklists, status), Wishlist, Saved Camps, Completed Trips, Reviews

**Quote:** *"Once I've decided where we're going, I don't want to lose track of it in a group chat — I want one place where the plan actually lives."*

---

## How these personas map to the product

| Persona | Primary pillars |
|---|---|
| Weekend Warrior | Discover, Search, Map, Reviews, Saved/Wishlist |
| Trip Reporter | Social (posts/photos), Profile, Follow graph |
| Community Builder | Communities, Moderation, Events |
| Planner | Trip Planner, Wishlist, Completed Trips |

Most real users will blend traits from more than one persona (e.g. a Trip Reporter who also organizes trips), but designing for these four distinct motivations ensures each core pillar has a clear, primary user to design for.
